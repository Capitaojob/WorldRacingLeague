World Racing League Regulations

Introduction

Welcome to the World Racing League (WRL), the premier racing organization for the high-speed, action-packed T-180 racing series inspired by the legendary Speed Racer. Our regulations are designed to ensure fair competition, safety, and the ultimate racing experience for fans and participants alike.

Race Format

  • Qualifying Rounds: Each race weekend begins with qualifying rounds to determine the starting grid. Drivers must complete the fastest lap times to secure their positions.
  • Main Race: The main race consists of multiple laps around a challenging circuit, with drivers competing for the top positions and championship points.
  • Pit Stops: Teams must manage strategic pit stops for refueling, tire changes, and repairs within designated pit areas.

Vehicle Regulations

  • T-180 Specifications: All racing vehicles must adhere to the WRL T-180 specifications, including engine capacity, weight limits, and safety features.
  • Modifications: Any modifications to the vehicles must be approved by the WRL technical committee. Unauthorized modifications will result in penalties or disqualification.
  • Safety Gear: Drivers must wear approved safety gear, including helmets, fire-resistant suits, and harnesses, at all times during the race.

Driver Conduct

  • Sportsmanship: All drivers are expected to display sportsmanship and respect towards fellow competitors, teams, and officials. However, aggressive driving and tactical collisions are part of the sport and encouraged.
  • On-Track Behavior: Drivers are allowed and expected to engage in aggressive maneuvers, including tactical collisions. Deliberate and unsafe driving that endangers lives will result in penalties, including fines, suspension, or disqualification.
  • Drug Testing: Random drug testing will be conducted throughout the season. Drivers found using banned substances will face immediate suspension and possible legal action.

Team Regulations

  • Team Registration: All teams must be registered with the WRL and comply with the league’s rules and regulations.
  • Team Personnel: Each team is allowed a maximum number of personnel, including drivers, engineers, and support staff, as specified by the WRL.
  • Sponsorship: Teams must disclose all sponsorship agreements and ensure that sponsors comply with WRL’s sponsorship guidelines.

Championship Points

  • Points System: Points are awarded based on race finishing positions. The driver and team with the most points at the end of the season are crowned champions.
  • Tiebreakers: In the event of a tie, the number of race wins and podium finishes will be used to determine the championship standings.

Appeals and Disputes

  • Appeal Process: Teams and drivers may appeal penalties and decisions made by WRL officials. Appeals must be submitted in writing within 24 hours of the incident.
  • Dispute Resolution: The WRL has a dedicated committee to handle disputes and ensure fair resolution in accordance with league regulations.

Updates and Amendments

The WRL reserves the right to update and amend these regulations as necessary. All teams and drivers will be notified of any changes, which will be effective immediately unless otherwise stated.
